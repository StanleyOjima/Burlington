Your field. Your pathway.

Every profession builds its EB-1A and NIW case differently. Burlington Consult develops petition strategies specific to how your field documents excellence.

The EB-1A criteria and NIW Dhanasar framework apply across all fields, but the evidence that satisfies them differs by profession. A surgeon’s case looks nothing like an engineer’s. Burlington Consult begins every engagement by identifying the strongest field of endeavour and criterion mapping for your specific record.

STEM Professionals

Engineering · Computer Science · Data Science · Research

Engineers and technologists at major firms and research institutions typically build cases around original contributions, scholarly output, and critical roles. Patent portfolios, open-source impact, and product-level contributions carry significant evidentiary weight.

Commonly mapped criteria
Original contributions of major significance in the field
Critical role at a distinguished organisation
High salary relative to peers in the field in relevant country
Scholarly articles in professional or major trade publications

Creative Professionals

Music · Film · Design · Visual Arts · Media

Creatives build cases through awards, published coverage in major media, commercial success, and display of work at recognised showcases. Burlington Consult works with the EB-1A for Creatives framework to map industry-specific achievements to USCIS criteria.

Commonly mapped criteria
Awards for excellence in the creative field
Published material about the petitioner in major media
Commercial success evidenced by sales, streaming, or distribution
Display of work at artistic exhibitions or showcases

Business & Finance

Investment · Consulting · Fintech · Private Equity · Strategy

Finance professionals and business leaders demonstrate extraordinary ability through high remuneration, critical leadership roles, original contributions to financial systems or market infrastructure, and membership in selective professional bodies.

Commonly mapped criteria
High salary relative to peers in the field in relevant country
Critical role at a distinguished organisation
Original contributions to financial systems or market infrastructure
Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
Start your assessment

Medicine & Healthcare

Clinical · Research · Public Health · Biomedical Science

Physicians and medical researchers build cases through peer-reviewed publications, journal reviewing activity, clinical innovations with documented patient impact, and recognition from national or international medical bodies. The NIW proposed endeavour is often tied to U.S. healthcare delivery or public health infrastructure.

Commonly mapped criteria
Scholarly articles in peer-reviewed medical journals
Judging: peer review for journals in the field
Original contributions advancing clinical practice or research
Awards for excellence in medicine or research
Start your assessment

Law & Policy

Legal Practice · Government · International Policy · Regulatory

Legal professionals and policy advisors demonstrate extraordinary ability through published scholarship, advisory roles in distinguished organisations, original contributions to regulatory frameworks, and membership in selective professional or government advisory bodies.

Commonly mapped criteria
Original contributions to legal or policy frameworks
Critical role in distinguished policy organisations
Published material about the petitioner in major publications
Membership in expert advisory councils or working groups
Start your assessment

Academia & Research

Faculty · Postdoctoral · Think Tanks · Research Institutions

Academics and researchers often hold the strongest publication and citation records, making scholarly output and peer review activity primary criteria. The NIW proposed endeavour typically frames the petitioner’s research programme as advancing a U.S. national interest in science, technology, or public health.

Commonly mapped criteria
Scholarly articles in peer-reviewed journals
Judging: peer review for journals or grant panels
Original contributions of major significance in the field
Membership in distinguished academic or research bodies
