The Strategist

Chris built his first immigration case the way most people wouldn't: on himself. Filing from Lagos with no lawyer, no agency, and no employer sponsor, he put together his own Einstein Visa (EB-1A) petition and submitted it to USCIS. It was approved on first submission. No Request for Evidence. No resubmission.

That experience changed everything. Not because the approval was rare (fewer than 5% of employment-based green cards carry the EB-1A classification) but because the process revealed how many strong candidates fail not on merit, but on argument. The evidence was there. The framework to present it wasn't.

Before founding Burlington Consult, Chris built a career that most people would spread across three lifetimes. Policy and integrity work at Meta Platforms. A $1.8 million project for the Gates Foundation. Responsible technology and AI strategy at Luminate, the Omidyar Network's policy arm. Government and regulatory affairs at Zipline. Corporate law at Templars, one of Nigeria's leading commercial law firms. Advisory at PwC. And a Google Policy Fellowship, the first ever awarded to someone from Africa.

He holds an LL.M. from Harvard Law School in Technology Law and AI Policy (Class of 2026), and has interviewed at final rounds at Harvard Business School, Stanford GSB, Wharton, OpenAI, Columbia, SpaceX, and the Tony Blair Institute. He has secured over $500,000 in scholarships from Harvard Law, Michigan Ross, Georgetown, USC, and Columbia.

Burlington Consult exists because Chris believes the system works, but only if the case is built correctly. Every engagement the firm takes starts from the same place his own petition did: the field of endeavour, stress-tested until it's bulletproof.
