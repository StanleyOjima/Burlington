Privacy Policy.

How Burlington Consult collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Last updated: March 2026

Introduction

Burlington Consult, LLC ("Burlington Consult," "we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at burlingtonconsult.com or engage our advisory services.

Information we collect

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Fill out a contact form on our website
  • Book and pay for a consultation session
  • Engage our advisory services under a client agreement
  • Communicate with us via email, phone, or WhatsApp

This information may include your name, email address, phone number, nationality, professional background, immigration history, educational qualifications, employment records, salary information, and any other information you choose to provide in connection with our advisory services.

How we use your information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Assess your eligibility for Einstein Visa (EB-1A), NIW (EB-2), and other U.S. immigration pathways
  • Develop and file immigration petitions on your behalf
  • Communicate with you regarding your engagement
  • Process payments for consultation and advisory services
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Improve our services and website functionality

Data sharing and disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We may share your information with:

  • U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) — as required for the preparation and filing of immigration petitions
  • Legal review partners — attorneys who provide independent legal review of petition materials, bound by confidentiality obligations
  • Payment processors — Stripe, Inc., which processes consultation and engagement payments on our behalf
  • Professional recommenders — individuals you identify as potential letter writers, contacted only with your express consent

Data security

We implement commercially reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Client materials are stored in access-controlled cloud environments with encryption at rest and in transit.

Data retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy any legal, regulatory, or reporting requirements. Immigration petition records are retained for a minimum of seven years following the conclusion of the engagement.

Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the processing of your personal information. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at founder@burlingtonconsult.com.

Cookies and analytics

Our website may use minimal analytics tools to understand site traffic and usage patterns. We do not use advertising cookies or tracking pixels. No personal information is shared with advertising networks.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was most recently revised. Your continued use of our website or services after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
