The Burlington Promise

The difference is never substance.
It's strategy.

Most people who enter competitive hiring processes are not underprepared on substance. They are underprepared on strategy. They do not know which opportunities to target, how to tell their story in a way that moves decision-makers, or what it actually takes to compete once they are in the room.

Burlington Consult's Career Advancement Advisory is a direct, personalised service for ambitious professionals who want to compete for the most selective roles, fellowships, and programmes available to them. We work with people who are transitioning careers, navigating the U.S. job market for the first time, recently laid off, or trying to break into the highest level of their field.

What We Do

The full campaign, end to end.

We work directly with clients from identifying which opportunities are worth pursuing to positioning your professional story, building your digital presence, developing a cold outreach strategy that actually generates responses, preparing you for the most demanding interview formats, and negotiating your offer once it comes.

This is not generic career coaching. It is built around the architecture of competitive hiring at elite organisations. That means understanding how companies like Meta, OpenAI, and Zipline think about candidates, what fellowship committees at programmes like Soros and Google Policy actually look for, and how to position yourself as the clear choice.

We specialise in high-achieving professionals from Africa and the Global South who are entering or advancing within the U.S. market. We understand the specific challenges of that journey, and we know what works.

Our scope: Opportunity identification, story architecture, resume and LinkedIn positioning, cold outreach strategy, networking plans, interview preparation across all formats, fellowship and selective programme applications, salary negotiation, and immigration strategy.

Your Adviser

The person behind your campaign.

Your engagement will be led directly by someone who has personally navigated and succeeded in some of the most selective hiring processes in the world.

Chukwuebuka "Ebuka" Ogbodo
LL.M. Candidate, Harvard Law School · EB-1A Holder

Ebuka graduated first class from the Nigerian Law School, finishing in the top three nationally. He became Africa's first Google Policy Fellow and built a career across three continents at the intersection of technology, law, and public policy.

His experience spans Public Policy Manager for Anglophone West Africa at Meta Platforms, Responsible Technology and AI Lead at Luminate (Omidyar Group), and transactional lawyer at Templars, where he advised on the $3B Nigeria LNG Train 7 expansion and the $1.3B Lekki Deep Sea Port financing.

He has interviewed at final rounds at HBS, Stanford, Wharton, Columbia, and MIT Sloan, and for senior roles at OpenAI, SpaceX Starlink, X, Bank of America, and the Tony Blair Institute.
