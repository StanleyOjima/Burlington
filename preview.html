A Partner-level position at a Tier 1 law firm, a senior role at an NSE-listed institution, a central bank appointment, or a decade at a Big Four firm in Lagos carries the same weight as the equivalent in London or New York. Why a Green Card

Access to U.S. market rates — without relocation.

A U.S. green card is permanent work authorisation, not a travel document. A senior professional in Lagos, London, or Nairobi who holds a green card can engage directly with U.S. clients, contract with U.S. institutions, and bill at U.S. rates — permanently.

A CBI passport provides travel advantages. It does not provide U.S. work authorisation.

Dual Citizenship

Multiple passports. One strategic question.

An increasing number of Burlington Consult's clients hold British, Canadian, Irish, or EU citizenships alongside their African nationalities.

Eligibility for EB-1A and NIW is based on professional merit, not nationality. The I-140 can be filed and approved regardless of any entry restriction on any passport you hold.

Practical example

A Nigerian-British dual citizen engaged Burlington Consult to obtain U.S. permanent residency as a career and compensation strategy. Senior financial services role in London, cross-border U.S. transactions. The British passport provided no advantage. Only U.S. permanent residency enabled direct engagement with U.S. clients at U.S. market rates. Filed under NIW (EB-2) — approved.

Core Competencies

What Burlington is best at.

Five areas where the firm's work is most differentiated — and where most other firms fall short.

01

Field of Endeavour Construction

The most consequential decision in any petition. Burlington Consult identifies and stress-tests the specific articulation of what the petitioner proposes to pursue in the United States — and why that work constitutes a national interest. Errors here are not correctable by adding stronger evidence later.

02

Dual Petition Strategy

Burlington Consult files EB-1A and EB-2 NIW petitions simultaneously as standard practice. Same priority date, shared evidence record, built-in redundancy. One engagement, two pathways, no wasted work.

03

Evidence Architecture

Curating, formatting, and presenting the full documentary record to the standard USCIS expects. Every exhibit cross-referenced to a specific criterion. Every claim backed by independent corroborating evidence sourced and verified by the team.

04

RFE Defence

RFE issuance rates are running at 41 to 49 percent in 2026. Unfavourable outcomes arise most often not from weak records but from inadequate responses. Burlington Consult includes a 12-month RFE and NOID retainer in every engagement at no additional cost.

05

STEM and Policy Crossover

Burlington Consult has particular depth in cases that sit at the intersection of technology, public policy, and regulatory affairs — fields where the proposed endeavour must be framed with precision to satisfy both the EB-1A criteria and the Dhanasar national interest test.
