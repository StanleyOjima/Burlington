For Artists, Musicians & Creatives

You might qualify for a U.S. Green Card.

No employer. No lottery. No relocation required. The U.S. Einstein Visa is available to artists, musicians, and creatives with exactly the kind of career you've been building.

The Opportunity

The EB-1A is not just for scientists.

Most successful African artists don't know this: the U.S. Einstein Visa, officially the EB-1A, reserved for people who have risen to the very top of their field, is available to artists, musicians, and creatives with exactly the kind of career you've been building.

You don't have to quit your current work. You don't have to move to America. You don't need an American employer, a visa lottery result, or a sponsor. You need your professional record, and the right team to document it.

What a U.S. Green Card Means for Your Career

Six practical advantages.

U.S. Touring Without Visa Anxiety. No more B-1/B-2 applications, no more ties to a single employer's sponsorship, no more tour dates at risk because of a processing backlog.

Direct Label and Management Deals. Work directly with U.S. labels, management companies, publishers, and streaming platforms as a permanent resident.

U.S. Business Formation. Open a U.S. entity, receive payments directly, structure international revenue through a U.S. vehicle with all the banking and tax advantages that creates.

Family Sponsorship. Bring your spouse and children to the U.S. as permanent residents. No separate visa applications, no separate sponsorship requirements.

Your Record Is Your Qualification. Awards, streaming numbers, media coverage, international recognition — these are the criteria you already meet.

The Window Is Now. A proposed rule change in 2026 will raise the evidentiary standard. Applications filed now are assessed against the current, more accessible standard.

How Artists Typically Qualify

Three or four criteria from the public record alone.

The EB-1A requires at least 3 of 10 regulatory criteria. For artists with a strong professional record, Burlington typically identifies the following:

Awards and Recognition — Grammy nominations, BET nominations, AFRIMA, Headies, MOBO, or other nationally or internationally recognised awards in the performing arts.

Published Material About the Artist — Features in Rolling Stone, Billboard, Variety, The Guardian, Pulse Nigeria, or equivalent major media. One substantive article is sufficient.

Commercial Success in the Performing Arts — Streaming numbers, concert revenue, album sales, international distribution deals. Burlington benchmarks against the relevant peer group.

Critical Role at a Distinguished Organisation — Headlining a major festival, leading a record label, or holding a creative director role at a recognised institution.
